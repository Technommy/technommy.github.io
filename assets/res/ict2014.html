General instructions

The second stage of application consists of two parts. Each part has a maximum word count that should not be exceeded. After each part, add your list of references. The list of references is not counted into the total word count.

You are free to use any method of reference, as long as it is clear in your text what ideas/information you have obtained from other sources. For example, referencing can be done by superscript1, numbering (2) or APA style (e.g. Johnson et al., 2013). In your list of references, these sources should then be presented (using links etc.)

Format

ICT-GROUP

Based on your application, you have been selected to the ICT group. Your two assignments are presented below.

PART 1: Who would you give the next Millennium Technology Prize to and why?

The Millennium Technology Prize is Finland's tribute to life-enhancing technological innovations that has a positive impact on the quality of life, both now and in the future. The aims of the prize are to promote technological research and innovation, alleviate fears towards technological change and encourage discussion between technology specialists and societal decision makers.

The prize is awarded every second year, and candidates are sought from across the world and all fields of technology.

For more information on the prize and the awarded innovations please visit http://www.technologyacademy.fi/millennium-technology-prize/

PART 2

Last year (2013) a Doppler measurement study of the star GJ 667C  22 light years away in the constellation Scorpius (http://www.eso.org/public/archives/releases/sciencepapers/eso1328/eso1328a.pdf) suggested 3 planets orbiting the star in the habitable zone, allowing liquid water on their surface and possible extraterrestrial life comparable to ours.

You are working in a group of multidisciplinary scientists for a mission to Gliese 667 Cc to check if the possible life of the planet is intelligent or not. This poses formidable challenges. The current blueprints for the vessel include a fusion propulsion engine (http://www.nasa.gov/pdf/716077main_Slough_2011_PhI_Fusion_Rocket.pdf http://deepblue.lib.umich.edu/bitstream/handle/2027.42/76963/AIAA-2009-4871-470.pdf?sequence=1) which may be able to provide enough acceleration and deceleration to reach the star system and its planet Gliese 667 Cc.

The answer to the question will be relayed to a satellite left on a very elliptical orbit around the star. The satellite will have three high yield thermonuclear bombs. They will be detonated with specific timing from each other to convey the answer to the question, at a time when the satellite is at a safe distance from the planet. The hope is that the blasts and their timing can be detected from Solar system satellites telescopes placed in multiple orbits that cover all times the view to the star unobstructed by the Sun. The synchronized three blasts with known time delays could be separated from the light of the star, if the satellite is on its apastron (the farthest position from the star on the satellite orbit). This is the only chance for the satellite to communicate back to us.

The payload will be very small, a communication system able to reach the orbiting satellite when it is close to the planet, and the most advanced artificial intelligence (AI) system to answer the question: Am I surrounded with intelligence? The current proposal has two buttons on the side of the lander. Red button with writing “Press me, if you are intelligent”, and a blue button with writing “Do not press me”. 

You are responsible for the AI system. The project management has requested a better system for the detection of intelligence. Propose one.

The maximum length for your proposal is two pages (max. 1000 words).
